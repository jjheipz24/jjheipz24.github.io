Coin Counter


Click on the image to see the program in action!

TASK

At BrickHack V, hosted by the Rochester Institute of Technology, we were all given 24 hours to create something from nothing. One of my friends (and teammates) was asked by a special education teacher to create an application that helps her students visualize money, specifically when giving change.

SOLUTION

We all took on separate parts: Julia Hotaling created all of the bills and coins using Photoshop and then used Three.js to make a 3D animation of the graphics on our site. Ella Mathewson designed and developed the site using HTML, CSS, Boostrap, animate.js, and jQuery. I used JavaScript, mathematics, and recursion to program the core functionality of the application.

Our program first contained a "Visualizer" page, where the user can enter an amount, and the program will show the corresponding bills and coins and the quantities of each.

The second part is the "Calculator" page, which dealt with giving change. The user enters the amount of the item, then clicks on the bills and coins they were given, and then the program shows how much change should be given and what is needed to get that amount.

The third part is a page that shows each bill and coin (front and back) and gives some identifying features and fun facts to help students recognize each one.

Software and Languages/Libraries used: JavaScript, HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, animate.js, Three.js, jQuery, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop
